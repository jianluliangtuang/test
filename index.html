About RCFD

The Ruangmei Christian Fellowship Delhi (RCFD) commenced its worship on February 6, 2011, at United Church, CNI RK Puram, Sector 6, New Delhi. However, the vision for its establishment had been long nurtured by devoted Ruangmei Christian leaders, notably the late Rev. Kabi Gangmei, who fervently prayed for a dedicated fellowship in Delhi. The RCFD was founded with a distinct purpose—to spiritually uplift and unify the Ruangmei community residing in the metropolitan expanse of Delhi. The establishment of RCFD not only served a religious purpose but also contributed to the cultural and social cohesion of the Ruangmei people living in a metropolitan environment like Delhi. It allowed the community to stay connected to their faith, traditions, and heritage, while also providing a space to address the challenges they faced as migrants in the city.

History of RCFD

RCFD operates under the Ruangmei Christian Cities Mission (RCCM) in association with the Ruangmei Naga Baptist Association (RNBA) and the Ruangmei Baptist Association, Nagaland (RBA). In its formative years, financial support from RNBA and RBA Nagaland contributed to the pastor's emoluments. However, through divine providence, RCFD has since achieved financial independence. Over the years, the fellowship has flourished, sustaining its ministries and various departments with unwavering commitment. Following the pastoral transition of Rev. Jaojian Gangmei to RNBA Centre Church, Imphal, Mr. Thuankulung Judah Pamei, then serving as Associate Pastor, was ordained as the Serving Pastor on March 1, 2020.

The divine vision and mission entrusted to RCFD have flourished, fostering spiritual growth and abundant blessings. Through God’s providence and unwavering support from the Ruangmei community, the fellowship has successfully acquired a plot in Burari for the establishment of Ruangmei House Delhi (RHD). Before the Covid-19 pandemic, congregational worship was held at Helen Jerwood Auditorium, St. Thomas Girls’ Sr. Secondary School, Mandir Marg. However, in response to changing circumstances, sermons and fellowship activities have transitioned to a virtual format, conducted from the RCFD office in Kishangarh, New Delhi.

CHURCH

We affirm that every believer is an integral member of the Church, the body of Christ, and that the local church is comprised of the redeemed. We uphold that, through the Holy Spirit, all believers whether Jew or Gentile are baptized into one unified body in Christ. Furthermore, we recognize the sacred duty of every local church to cultivate unwavering love and spiritual unity with a pure heart (Eph.1: 20-23; 4:3-10); .

SACRAMENTS

Sacraments are sacred rites instituted by Christ, serving as outward signs of inward grace. Water Baptism symbolizes spiritual rebirth and initiation into the faith, while the Lord’s Supper commemorates Christ’s sacrifice and fosters unity among believers. These holy ordinances not only affirm one's commitment to God but also embody the church’s witness in the world (Matt. 28:19; Acts 10:47-48; 16:32-33; 18:7-8;).

GODHEAD

The doctrine of the Trinity reveals the profound mystery of God’s nature—three co-equal, co-eternal persons united in perfect harmony. The Father is the Creator and sustainer, the Son is the incarnate Word and Redeemer, and the Holy Spirit is the divine presence guiding and empowering believers. Though distinct in personhood, they are one in essence, will, and glory, affirming the unity and majesty of the Triune God.

SCRIPTURE

God’s Word is living and active, sharper than any two-edged sword, discerning the thoughts and intentions of the heart (Hebrews 4:12). It is eternal and unchanging, standing firm in the heavens (Psalm 119:89) and serving as a lamp to our feet and a light to our path (Psalm 119:105). Every prophecy, promise, and command in Scripture is God-breathed, making it profitable for teaching, rebuking, correcting, and training in righteousness.

MAN AND HIS FALL
